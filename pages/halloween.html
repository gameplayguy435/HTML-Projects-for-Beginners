Halloween

Halloween is a celebration observed in various countries, primarily in the English-speaking world, on October 31st, the eve of the Western Christian feast of All Saints' Day. It begins with the three-day vigil of Allhallowtide, the time of the liturgical year dedicated to remembering the dead, including saints (hallows), martyrs, and all the faithful departed.

Story

The origin of Halloween traces back to the traditions of people who inhabited Gaul and the British Isles between 600 BC and 800 AD, although with notable differences from the current pumpkins or the very famous phrase "trick or treat", exported by the United States, which popularized the celebration. Originally, Halloween had no connection to witches. It was a festival in the Celtic calendar of Ireland, the Samhain festival, celebrated between October 30 and November 2, and marked the end of summer (samhain literally means "end of summer").
